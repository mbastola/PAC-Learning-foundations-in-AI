\section{PAC Semantics and Resolution}

The scribe and homework materials connect learning-theoretic approximation with
logical entailment. Classical entailment requires a conclusion to hold in every
model satisfying the premises. PAC semantics relaxes this: a rule may fail on a
small probability mass under the data distribution.

For a premise $p$ and conclusion $q$, the executable version in this repository
checks the finite-distribution condition
\[
  \Pr_{x \sim \D}[p(x) \Rightarrow q(x)] \geq 1-\epsilon.
\]

The resolution material is represented just as a tiny propositional helper. A
clause is a set of signed integers: $1$ means $x_1$, and $-1$ means $\neg x_1$.
Resolving $\{x\} \cup A$ with $\{\neg x\} \cup B$ produces $A \cup B$.

This keeps the logic side present without making the whole repository a logic
programming project.
